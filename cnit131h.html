A little information on Chardonnay and Sauvignon Blanc

Chardonnay

The Chardonnay grape is one of the most well known grapes.

Chardonnay grapes is easily manipulated for the different styles of Chardonnay wines.

Chardonnay wines can be big full-bodied wines, fermented in oak to give a buttery mouthfeel with toasty oak flavors of vanilla and butterscotch.
Or it can be fermented in stainless steel to give a more focused, linear and crisp style of wine.

No matter what direction the winemaker decides to take the wine, Chardonnay will always potray characters of apples and pears.
That is how Sommeliers pinpoint a chardonnary in a blind tasting.

Sauvignon Blanc

The Sauvignon Blanc grape is also very well known, it is a very popular selction today.

Sauvignon blanc can be fermented in oak, but most styles are in stainless steel to showcase the fruit characteristics of the wine.

Sauvignon blanc's tell tell sign in a blind tasting is grapefruit!

Sanvignon Blanc is very well known in the Loire Valley of France, as Sancerre. A more sharp and crisp wine, bursting with citrus notes.
